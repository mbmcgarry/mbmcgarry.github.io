I am

a Postdoctoral Fellow

... at the University of Wisconsin-Madison in the Computational Nuclear Engineering Research Group. I am funded by the NNSA Consortium for Verification Technology.

Currently

... I am developing a model to compare the relative risk of nuclear material diversion in different fuel cycles using the CYCLUS nuclear fuel cycle simulator.

My research interest

... is at the interface between science and policy. I seek to provide technical analysis of policy-relevant issues, such as creating a safe, emissions-free, sustainable nuclear fuel cycle with minimal risk of nuclear proliferation.

My Ph.D.

... in experimental plasma physics was received in 2013 from the University of Wisconsin-Madison. There, I worked with Daniel den Hartog and Paolo Franz on the Madison Symmetric Torus to develop a soft x-ray tomography and double-filter diagnostic. I used this diagnostic to study the magnetic confinement and the electron temperature structure of high-current plasmas on MST. After completing my PhD, I spent one year as a postdoc at MST characterizing the effects of impurities on the x-ray transmission properties of beryllium foils.

My policy experience

... began at an International Summer Symposium for Science and World Affairs hosted by the Union of Concerned Scientists, where I was introduced to the field of international security policy. Since that time, I have participated in two additional symposia, presenting original research in collaboration with Dr. Laura Grego on the feasibility of developing a high-powered microwave weapon for use as an anti-satellite weapon in space. I have also written a white paper in collaboration with the Natural Resources Defense Council that examines the recent progress of the National Ignition Facility and the likelihood that it, as the most expensive attempt at inertial confinement fusion, will achieve ignition.

My undergraduate degree

...is in physics and astrophysics from the University of California, Berkeley. While at Berkeley, I used my Cal Space Summer Fellowship to look for optical signals from aliens in the SETI group. I also spent one summer in the REU astronomy program at the University of Hawaii at Manoa, where I used spectral modeling to determine galactic redshifts.

My research experience

... also includes three years as a data analyst for the Science Mission Planning group at the Chandra X-ray Observatory. As a mission planner, I scheduled observations and wrote code in Perl to maintain related websites and databases. I also researched the observational properties of Anomalous X-ray Pulsars. You can learn more about my hodgepodge assortment of previous work on my CV.
